About BISU Calape Campus...

Bohol Island State University - Calape (BISU - Calape) is a public university campus located in Calape, Bohol and is one of the external campuses part of the Bohol Island State University (BISU) System. The campus started out as a community college and was eventually converted into a polytechnic college in 1989 by the virtue of Republic Act 7370.

The Calape campus houses eight (8) academic degree programs that are composed of undergraduate and technical-vocational courses. Its undergraduate programs include bachelor's degrees in Elementary Education, Secondary Education, and Industrial Technology, which all have Level II AACCUP accreditation. While other programs include degrees in Fisheries, and Computer Science, which all have Level I accreditation.

HISTORY OF BISU CALAPE CAMPUS

The Bohol Island State University comes into existence through grit and determination of its leaders.

It started from a humble community college founded by the late Mayor Anunciacion R. Tuazon, and originally named Calape Community-Based Agro Industrial College (CCBAIC) in June 1989. Later, CCBAIC was converted into a polytechnic college known as Calape Polytehnic College (CPC) by virtue of Republic Act of 7370. In June 1998, Republic Act 8659 established Central Visayas State College of Agriculture, Forestry, and Technology out of the integration of the five (5) chartered colleges all in the province of Bohol except CPC. CPC was then integrated into CVSCAFT System on October 30, 1998 through BOT Resolution No. 2 Series of 1998 adopting the college.

Finally, on October 14, 2009 CVSCAFT evolved anew with the name Bohol Island State University by Virtue of Republic Act 9722 converting the college, its unit and satellite campuses into a University.
