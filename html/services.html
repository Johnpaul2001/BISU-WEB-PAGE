Student Welfare and Development Services


A. LIBRARY


I. General Rules


1. To ensure the smooth operation of the library the following guidelines are set:

    *Upon entering the library, all users must present their validated school ID/library card.


    *The school ID/library card is presented every time a library material is borrowed/returned. These cards are non-transferable. Tampered cards will not be honored.


    *The loss of a library card should be reported immediately to the Circulation Counter. The student will have to secure another library card.


2. Defacing, mutilating and stealing of library materials are strictly prohibited.


3. Upon leaving the library, all users must present their things for inspection.


4. The library is mainly for research. Eating, smoking, vandalism, sleeping and group discussion are not allowed.


5. Silence must be observed strictly at all times. Cellphones and other devices should be in a silent mode inside the library.


6. The library should be kept clean and orderly.


7. Newspapers and magazines should be read inside the library.


8. Books should be left on the table after use.


9. Library materials should be handled with care. Marking, underlining, writing, inserting bulking objects and making "dog ears" are prohibited.


10. Chairs should be pushed back quietly before leaving.


11. Damaged and lost books must be replaced.


12. Fines will be imposed for overdue books based on the library user's manual.


13. Renewal is strictly made only when the books are physically brought to the library.


14. Non-BISU researchers and outsiders can access the library and will be charged a research fee of P50.00.


15. Courtesy should be observed at all times when borrowing abd returning books or other materials.



II. Library Hours


Monday to Friday - 7:30 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. No noon break.


Saturday - 8:00 to 12:00 noon & 1:00 p.m. to 5:00 p.m.



B. GUIDANCE SERVICES


1. Individual Inventory Services


~ This services involves the acquisition, maintenance and safeguarding of individual records of student. It aims to gather, organize and compile pertinent data and information of every student for its functional use. Such records include personal and educational data, test results and other related information that are useful for the development of the client's educational, emotional, spiritual, and social well-being.


1.a All freshmens, returness and transferees should have a Guidance Inventory Record which contains the result of the entrance, differential aptitude, interest, diagnostic & special needs inventory tests.


1.b Student's Inventory Records should be used as a reference for the development of ones educational, emotional, spiritual, and social well-being.


2. Information Services


~ This services encompasses all information which are made available through bulletins, announcements, brochures, electronic media, orientation activities and trainings.


Guidance Center shall be disseminate relevant information for the holistic development of the clients.


2.a All freshmen and transferees are required to attend the orientation program at the beginning of each year.


2.b Orientation programs should include activities meant to meet students' need for adjustment.


2.c Pertinent press information are made available through bulletins, announcements & brochures.


3. Counseling Services


~ This service is concerned with providing individual and group counseling to students who voluntarily visit the Guidance Center to seek assistance, to 'referrals' given by administrators, family members, parents and faculty members who are in need of assistance that could affect the student's educational, emotional, spiritual and social well-being.


3.a Students can access to individual and group counseling services provided by the Guidance Counselor.


3.b Counseling service can be availed personally, by phone, through the internet or through the printed media.


4. Testing Service


~ This service includes the administration and interpretation of tests to assess the performance and personality of the students. Students who wish to avail the testing services may make arrangements with the guidance counselor.


4.a Students should be avail of the guidance program's mental ability, aptitude, diagnostic & personality tests at the Guidance Center.


4.b Students should be informed of the tests results.


5. Placement Services


~ The activities in this service include the establishment of job placement linkages with the different firms, agencies and others, providing maximum information on the employment opportunities not only to graduating students but also to the non-graduating.


5.a Students should be provided with information/educational and employments opportunities.


5.b Career Guidance to students will include their classification according to aptitude & interests.


5.c Student trainees should be given orientation prior to OJT deployment by the Placement Officer.


5.d Creating and sustaining linkages with the alumni and industries for the job opportunities.


6. Follow-up Service


~ It is concerned with what happens to the students while in school and keeps track of them in finding out how students fair in their chosen activity.


6.a Students who availed of the guidance services shall be monitored in their performance.


6.b All graduating students should undergo an exit conference with the Guidance Counselor.


6.c All graduating students should fill up the Graduates Directory Form.



C. STUDENT SCHOLARSHIP


In order to avail of any of the scholarship grants, the following guidelines should be observed.


1. One must be a regular student.


2. He must not have any derogatory record.


3. He must carry a regular load.


4. Application forms must be submitted to the office of the scholarship coordinator.


OTHER SCHOLARSHIPS


1. The school may be accepted endorsement grants from any identified sponsoring agency/ies be it from government and non-government agencies.


2. Scholars from any sponsoring agency shall submit to the school proof of that scholarship.


3. Scholars are subject to the policy and standards promulgated by the sponsoring agency and delivering institution.



D. MEDICAL/DENTAL SERVICES


1. All students can avail of free medical and dental services.


2. Every student must be a member of the Student Medicare System. Upon payment of the membership fee he/she can of the medical aid within the school year for secondary/semester for college.


3. Medical aid can be availed of in case of accidents occuring:


a. in the campus during class hours.


b. outside the school while undertaking school related activities.


c. in going to and from the school before and after class hours.


4. The membership fee/medical aid may increase from year to year as determined by the PTGEAI/PGFEA Board.



E. SCHOOL CAFETERIA


1. Students are encouraged to patronize the products and services offered by the cafeteria.


2. Students are not allowed to eat inside the classrooms and offices.


3. Students are prohibited from studying inside the cafeteria.


4. Those who bring their own provisions of lunch/snacks are responsible for cleaning the table and throwing the garbage in the trash cans.



F. STUDENT ASSISTANTSHIP PROGRAM


Any bonafide students of BISU can avail of the student assistantship program during his/her periods and off-schools house with a maximum rate per hour as prescribed by the BOR not more than 4 hours a day and 8 hours on Saturdays, Sundays and holidays but not exceed 100 hours per month within the semester.


1. The applicant must secure the application form from the Student Affairs Office and ask recommendation from the Project In-charge/office head. The SAO will endorse the application to the Campus Director for approval.


2. The official time for duty will be during their free periods and off-hours.


3. Student assistant who renders services in the evening should be up to 9:00 pm. Should there be a need of extra service, he may be allowed to stay/sleep in school.


4. Student assistant should be assigned in line with his course specialization as the case maybe.


5. Student Assistant shall be paid monthly.



G. STUDENT ORGANIZATION


1. Only bonafide student of BISU may organize any Campus Student Organization.


2. All Campus Student Organization (CSO) must be duly screened, registered, recognized and accredited by the Dean of Student Affairs.


3. Campus Organizations must comply with all the Student Affairs Office (SAO) requirements for registration, recognition and accredition.


4. A newly formed organization shall be recognized on probation basis for at least one school year.


5. Full recognition of an organization shall be based on the evaluation conducted by the Committee on Student Organization headed by the Dean of SAO, SSG Adviser and Guidance Counselor.


6. Existing organizations who wish to renew their recognition must submit the documents for renewal.


7. Activities and program sponsored, spearheaded and organized by the CSO for academic enrichment, cultural purposes, fund raising campaigns and educational/field trips, within and outside of the campus must secure activity permits from the SAO and Department Deans. It shall recommendation by College Dean and Student Affairs.


8. Approved activity of CSO must be submitted to the SAO at least two weeks before the activity.


9. CSO, posters, announcements and streams must be approved by the SAO for posting. These must be posted only at designated places and bulletin boards.


10. Posters, announcements, streamers should be removed after serving the purposes.


11. An elected/appointed CSO president can no longer be a president of another organization.



H. MULTI-FAITH SERVICES


1. The institution should provide an environment conducive to free expression of one's religious orientation in accordance with institutional principles & policies.


2. This will be managed by the Director of Student Affairs in coordination with the different religious organizations of the institution.


* Coordinates services of other faith groups in organizing social events and inter-faith dialogue.


* Assits students to find the appropriate venues in organizing activities promoting spiritual campus life.


* An ecumenical service should be iniated at least once a semester.



I. TRADE SKILLS TESTING PROGRAM


1. The TESDA (Technical Education and Skills Development Authority) conducts a yearly Trade Skill Test to BISU graduating students in the designed testing center of skills accreditation.


2. The Trade Skills Test comes in two phases: Knowledge and Skills. For the skills test, the examinee will provide his own materials.


3. The applicant fills up the appropriate form and pays the corresponding trade testing fee to TESDA.

Student Affairs and Services



OBJECTIVES:


BISU Student Affiars and Services Programs are crafted to:


* raise a well-informed student-clientele;


* provide guidance and counseling services that shall help form the fullest potential of students and maintain the standard of the university through its admission and retention programs;


* ensure fitness of graduates to the jobs that they are designed to work in the society;


* instill the value of economic ventures and entrepreneurial spirit among students;


* revise existing handbook for development sake tto suit to the needs of the students;


* raise leaders who are inncorruptible and equipped with leadership skills and sensitivity to social responsibility;


* inculcate the value of discipline among students;


* hone student writers to become responsible journalist and enjoy freedom of press;


* make available to students services which are deemed needed for students holistic development such as scholarship and financial assistance, food services and health services;


* ensure a safe and secured campus and student life; and


* enkindle the spirit of sportmanship among students and cause a culture-appreciative BISUANS.




THE STUDENT SERVICES PROGRAM HAS THE FOLLOWING COMPONENTS:



1. STUDENT WELFARE


* Information and Orientation Services. This service encompasses all information which is made available through bulletins, announcements after flag rituals, brochures, electronic media, orientation activities and trainings. Student Affairs Office shall disseminate relevant information for the holistic development of the clients.


* Guidance Services and Counseling Services. This service is concerned with providing individual and group counseling to students who seek assistance, the "referrals" who are being referred by administrators, family members, parents and teachers who are in need of assistance that could affect the student's educational, emotional, spiritual and well-being. It aims to help the individual to develop potentials and become aware of limitations which are vital in making a decisions and right choices in life.


* Career and Job Replacement Services. This service looks after the placement of graduates after graduation through the conduct of Career Orientation, joining Jobs Fairs and Special Recruitment Activities. This maintains linkages with industries for OJT and employment purposes. While it seeks linkage for employment it takes care of the Job Preparation Trainings among graduating students.


* Economic Enterprise Development. This service is designed to inculcate among students the beauty and essence of entrepreneurial activities. It encourages individuals and groups to conduct entrepreneurial endeavors thrrough orientation programs and proposal making seminar.


* Student Handbook Development. This student welfare service sees to it that stakeholders, namely: students, instructors, deans, administrators, parents and alumni are involved in the revisions of the student handbook.



2. STUDENT DEVELOPMENT


* Student Activities. Student Activities are calendared, circulated and posted for information and guide purposes.


* Student Organization and Activities. These are made available to bonafide students of the BISU. The student organizations and their activities are geared towards the total development of the students. To come up with the efficient and effective management of the student organization, advisers are appointed by the Campus Director. There are set of officers who are tasked to manage and carry out all activities undertaken.


* Leadership Training. The production of incorruptible and empowered future leaders of the country is the aim of this service. This service is provided both by the Office of the Supreme Student Government and the Office of the Chair of Student Affairs and Services.


* Student Council/Government. This office is manned by the elected student leaders and their adviser. The government is patterned after the current set up of the Philippine Government. Here they have the following officials: SSG President and Vice President, Senators, and Representatives, College Governors and Vice Governors are also elected by their respective colleges. Class Mayors are also existent in the respective sections.


* Student Discipline. A judicious implementation of institutional rules and regulation governing student behavior is the look out of this function. This pertains to the disciplinary measures imposed to students who violate schools and regulations, within and outside of the school premises. The Student Disciplime Action Committee or SDAC is the investigating body who has the jurisdiction to try cases involving student/s in accordance with the rules and regulations stipulated in the Student handbook and other applicable rules and regulations. The SDAC is composed of the following: Director of Student Affairs and Services, Civil Security Ofiicer, Dean of the Complainant and of the Person Complained Of, SSG Adviser and Academic Adviser.


* Student Publication/ Year Book. This institution has a student official publication/organ/journal, here at the BISU Balilihan Campus known as "CAMPUS ACCESS". It covers the management of the School Paper, the duties and responsibilities of the editorial staff, conduct of journalism workshop and the publication of the school paper.



3. INSTITUTIONAL STUDENT PROGRAMS AND SERVICES


* Admission Services. The admission services of BISU Balilihan CAmpus are primarily handled by the Office of the Student Affairs and Servoces, where the Chairperson of the Student Affairs spearheads the management of entrance examination. Aiding the office are the faculty designated as proctors and facilitators during admission period.


* Scholarship and Financial Assistance (SFA). BISU has internally and externally funded scholarship. in BISU Balilihan, among the internally funded scholarship are as follows: DILG Grantees, Non Academic Scholarship (SSG), For the Externally Funded Scholarship they are categorized as follows: CHED, other Government Agencies such as DOST and LGU's; and from private institutions such as Tzu Chi Foundation and OFWDSP.


* Food Services. The food service shall cater to the nutritional needs of the students, faculty, employees, administrators and guests. The service provides clean, safe and quality food at a reasonable price to all clienteles. The services is managed by Cafeteria/Canteen In-charge, Food Technology Teachers and Canteen personnel in coordination with the Health personnel, Director of Student Affairs, and Collge Dean


* Health and Wellness Services. The health services offered by the university and made available to all bonafide students including the teachning and non-teaching personnel. The services cover medical and dental check-up, administration of initial doses of medication, wound dressing, tooth extraction and prophylaxis and other minor cares that need medical/dental attention. The managment and opeartion of the health services are confined within the responsibility of the medical/dental personnel of the university. Cases beyond the capacity of the university are referred to hospitals.


* Safety and Security Services. The safety and security services is headed by the Chief Security Officer who directly reports to the Campus Director coordinates with the Director of Student Affairs and Services for programs. Licensed Security Officers are hired via security agency to watch the security of the campus. Regular fire and earthquake drills are also conducted.


* Student Housing and Residential Services. Though the University for the mean time does not have available space for student housing, the Student Affairs and Services Office tend to connect with the boarding houses nearby.


* Multi-faith and Inter-faith Services. BISU ensures that it shall provide an environment conducive to freely express their religious orientation in accordance with institutional principles and policies. It ensures that the right of freddom of religion is respected through the coordination with the different religious organization. Each of these registered religious student organizations is given time to share a certain values in a "Monday-Value Sharing" after the Flag Raising Ceremony.


* Services for Students with Special Needs and persons with Disabilities. This service is provided for the persons with disabilities and even with people of minor defects such as near and far sightedness, orthopedics, cases of hyper ventilation and others.


* Culture and Arts Programs and Sports and Development Programs. BISU ensures that the physical fitness and wellness programs are given to students. The sports activities include in-campus, inter-campus, provincial, regional and national competitions. The managment and operation of the sports and socio-cultural activities are managed by the Coordinator of Sports and Cultural Affairs.


* Social and Community Involved Programs. This program is intended to carry out the social responsibility of the university while students develop their skills.


*Research and Monitoring. Research and monitoring related to student welfare, development and services are conducted to assess the services provided for improvement sake.
