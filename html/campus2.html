About BISU Main Campus...

Bohol Island State University (BISU) is a state university system located in Bohol, established in 2009 through the Rupublic Act 9722 signed by former president Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo. Under the said law, Central Visayas State College of Agriculture, Forestry, and Technology (CVSCAFT) and five other technical-vocational schools in the province will be merged to form the current BISU. The university's main campus is situated in Tagbilaran City.

While the other satellite campuses are located in the municipalities of Candijay, Bilar, Calape, Balilihan, and Clarin. The academic institution offers for students from all over the Visayas Region. Undergraduate and grdauate programs in Education, Agriculture, Fisheries, Engineering, Arts and Sciences, Accountancy, Business, and Technology are readily available to individuals who want to pursue their education in the university. Also offered is a Senior High School program featuring Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) Strands.


HISTORY OF BISU MAIN CAMPUS

In 1907, it was started with a bamboo and nipa shack that housed the class composed of a handful Grade Five boys taking industrial arts in woodworking. The class was an extension of the Tagbilaran Elementary School. The teacher was an American named George W. Jackson. Years later the class was attached as industrial arts of Bohol Provincial High School.

In 1914, Bohol Trade School was established as separate school from the Provincial High School with an initial enrollment of 110 pupils. It began offering the Secondary Trade Curriculum along with the Intermediate Course.

In 1932, the intermediate trade curriculum was dropped. From then on, the school offered the Secondary Trade Curriculum. The first course offered was woodworking, followed by Building Construcations and Automechanics. The enrollment reached a little less than 500 until the outbreak of World War II.

BISU, and its program offerings are duly recognized by the Cmmission on Higher Education (CHED), the Department of Education (DepEd), and the Tecghnical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA).

Since its establishments, Bohol Island State University has been consistently providing a low-cost and high-quality education to its students and molding them into globally-competent and skillful individuals who will bring progress to the province and entire country.

BISU - Main Campus - formerly The Bohol School of Arts and Trade was established in 1906. It started as Bohol Trade School and was converted into a school of arts and trades in 1959. It is located along the CPG Avenue, Tagbilaran City. BISU Main Campus - Bingag Extension - located in Bingag, Dauis, 15 kilometers away from Tagbilaran City. It was established in 2009.





HISTORY OF BISU MAIN CAMPUS

In 1968, the school was selected as a Pilot Training Center of Manpower Training Program under the National Manpower and Youth Council.

In 1975, it was authorized to offer the four-year Bachelor of Science in Industrial Technology.

In 1979, the tragedy struck the school on March 24 when the fire razed it to ashes. Picking up from the debris of the burned buildings, the school began reconstruction work. In five years, the school rose again with the three new buildings.

In 1988, it was authorized to offer Technology Courses.

In 1991, it was authorized to offer Bachelor of Secondary Education major in THE. Specialization of the school in Civil, Electrical, Refrigeration & Airconditioning and Welding

HISTORY OF BISU MAIN CAMPUS

In 1948, the four-year Trade course was restored, at the same time keeping the two-year course which was popularly known as The Evening Trade Opportunity Trade Classes (EOTC).

In 1949, the school became co-educational. Female students had a choice of dressmaking, food trades or handicrafts

In 1950, the FOA-PHILCUSA aid program became came to rehabilitate the school. New equipment and hand tools were recieved to improve the school facilities.

In 1959, under the Omnibus Act, Bohol Trade School was converted into Bohol of School of Arts and Trades. The school offered two-year technical education curriculum, four-year secondary school and EOTC.
