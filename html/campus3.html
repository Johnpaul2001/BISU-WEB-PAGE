About BISU Candijay Campus...

Bohol Island State University - Candijay (BISU - Candijay) is a state-owned university campus located in Cogtong, Candijay, Bohol. Formerly known as the Bohol School of Fisheries (BSF), the school later became part of the Central Visayas State College of Agriculture, Forestry, and Technology (CVSCAFT)s system, whisch is presently known as the Bohol Island State University.

BISU - Candijay Campus offers undergraduate and graduate degree programs in Fisheries, Marine Biology, Education,Tourism,Computer Science,Environmental Resource Management, and Hotel and Restaurant Services. Aside from undergraduate and graduate programs, the university also accepts senior high school students. Strands under the Technical - Vocational - Livelihood (TVL) track are taught to them.


BISU Candijay Campus Profile



Date Established:

1948 - Candijay High School (CHS)
1959 - Bohol School of Fisheries (BSF)
1998 - Central Visayas State College of Agriculture, Forestry and Technology (CVSCAFT)
2009 at present - Bohol Island State University (BISU)


Land Area:

30.95 hectares campus site including fishpond.

Throughout the years, BISU - Candijay has been continuing to provide young Boholanoes with quality instructions and training which could help them develop into productive and effective individuals who can contribute to national development, especially in agriculture and fisheries.



The Bohol Island State University (BISU) formerly the Central Visayas State College of Agriculture, Forestry and Technology (CVSCAFT) public institution of higher learning in Bohol, Philippines. The institution operates campuses spread throughout the province, with main campus in Tagbilaran City, Bohol.



By virtue of Republic Act no. 9722 ( An Act Converting the CVSCAFT, Its Units and Satellite Campuses) in the city of Ragbilaran and in the Municipalities in Bilar, Candijay, Calape, Clarin and Balilihan located in the Province in Bohol to be known as the Bohol Isalnd State University.
